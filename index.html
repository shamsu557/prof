Professor Francis Ibeawuchi Amagwu, HCIB, FAHI, WAAD

Amagwu, Ibeawuchi Francis (fondly called Frank) was born in Kafanchan (Kaduna State) and hails from Aboh Mbaise in Imo State. He graduated from the University of Calabar with a B.Sc. (Hons) Degree, Second Class Upper Division in Banking and Finance. He was the best graduating student in the Department of Banking and Finance in 1990 and a recipient of the IBWA Bank Award for Academic Excellence in 1990. He immediately proceeded to the University of Lagos, Akoka, to earn a Masterâ€™s of Science (M.Sc.) degree in Finance and later went to the University of Nigeria, Nsukka, where he earned a second M.Sc. degree in Developmental Studies with distinction. Thereafter, he bagged a Ph.D. in Development Finance (also with distinction) from the University of Nigeria Nsukka.

An Associate Research Fellow at the African Institute of Applied Economics (AIAE), now African Heritage Institution, Enugu, Ibeawuchi (Frank) Amagwu was the Chairman of Enugu Forum (2006 to 2010). He is a Fellow of the Nigerian Institute of Treasury and Financial Accountants (FTFA) as well as a member of the Nigerian Institute of Management (MNIM) and also belongs to the World Association of Academic Doctors (WAAD) (Charity Organization, Maryland, USA). He is an honorary member of the Chartered Institute of Bankers of Nigeria (HCIB).

Frank started his career after graduation with the Federal Airport of Nigeria (FAAN) as an Internal Auditor and later entered into commercial banking practice with Commerce Bank Plc. He also worked with FSB International Bank Plc, briefly with Fidelity Bank PLC before joining First City Monument Bank Plc in 2006, where he worked as Regional Director East on Assistant General Manager grade. He left commercial banking practice after nineteen years to join the Central Bank of Nigeria as Assistant Director in April 2011.

He served as the Head of Development Finance at the Central Bank of Nigeria, Enugu from 2011-2016, Associate Head of the Micro, Small and Medium Enterprise Financing Division (MSMEFD), and later as Head of MSMEDF before his appointment as Branch Controller of Central Bank, Abuja Branch. An inspirational leader, motivational speaker, banker, and development finance expert, he is the author of philosophical books like "Life Is Pain" and "The Fool in Me"; has also published several articles on microfinance, development finance, and contemporary issues in both local and international journals like SAGE journal.

Married to Dr. Chinedum Christabel, he is blessed with two children. Ibeawuchi (Frank) Amagwu is a faculty member of Apollos University, Montana, USA, and an Associate Professor in Micro and Development Finance. He is also a facilitator with the National Open University of Nigeria (NOUN), currently supervising 10 postgraduate candidates (MSc and MBA) at the Garki Centre.

Present Employment and Responsibilities: Ibeawuchi Amagwu currently works as the Branch Controller at the Central Bank of Nigeria, Abuja Branch, a position he has held since 2018.
