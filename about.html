Professor Amagwu Ibeawuchi Francis

Professor Amagwu Ibeawuchi Francis, fondly called Frank, was born in Kafanchan, Kaduna State, and hails from Aboh Mbaise, Imo State. He holds a B.Sc. (Hons) in Banking and Finance from the University of Calabar, graduating as the best student in his department in 1990 and receiving the prestigious IBWA Bank Award for Academic Excellence.

He furthered his education at the University of Lagos, Akoka, where he earned an M.Sc. in Finance, followed by another M.Sc. in Development Studies (with distinction) from the University of Nigeria, Nsukka. He later attained his Ph.D. in Development Finance, also with distinction, from the University of Nigeria.

Frank is an Associate Research Fellow at the African Heritage Institution (formerly African Institute of Applied Economics, AIAE), where he served as Chairman of the Enugu Forum (2006–2010). He is a Fellow of the Nigerian Institute of Treasury and Financial Accountants (FTFA) and a member of the Nigerian Institute of Management (MNIM). Additionally, he belongs to the World Association of Academic Doctors (WAAD) in Maryland, USA, and is an honorary member of the Chartered Institute of Bankers of Nigeria (HCIB).

His career journey began at the Federal Airport Authority of Nigeria (FAAN) as an Internal Auditor. He then transitioned into commercial banking with Commerce Bank Plc and later worked with FSB International Bank Plc, Fidelity Bank Plc, and First City Monument Bank Plc, where he held the position of Regional Director (East). In 2011, Frank joined the Central Bank of Nigeria as an Assistant Director, where he served in various roles, including Head of Development Finance, Head of MSMEDF, and Branch Controller of the CBN Abuja Branch since 2018.

Frank has authored philosophical works such as "Life Is Pain" and "The Fool in Me" and published several research articles on microfinance and development finance in journals like SAGE. He is married to Dr. Chinedum Christabel, and they are blessed with two children.

Professor Amagwu is currently a faculty member at Apollos University, Montana, USA, and an Associate Professor at the National Open University of Nigeria (NOUN). He supervises MSc and MBA postgraduate candidates at NOUN’s Garki Centre.
